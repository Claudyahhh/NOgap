# Retention Classification

## Confirmed offers

Do not rely on the token `offer` alone; it also appears in unrelated prose.

Retain a message as a confirmed offer when at least one strong signal exists:

- subject contains `offer letter`, `internship offer`, `employment offer`, `录用通知`, `聘用通知`, or `入职通知`;
- sender is a recruiting or HR identity and the body confirms selection;
- an attached offer document is clearly named as such.

Sample search results before treating them as retained messages.

## Official role accounts

An organization-owned domain is not enough. Named employees, teachers, supervisors, and students are personal senders even when they use an organizational domain.

Strong official-role signals include:

- `admissions`, `registry`, `faculty office`, `student services`;
- `careers`, `finance`, `library`, `IT service desk`, `security`;
- `human resources`, `recruiting`, `press office`, `support`;
- government department, public authority, or institutional service identities.

Use sender display name, address, subject, and message purpose together. Preserve ambiguous messages and report them unless the user selected "keep nothing."

## Flags and favorites

Flags are user-authored metadata. Do not toggle them to encode temporary cleanup state.

If a previous run already mixed temporary and original flags:

1. Stop and explain the loss of provenance.
2. Retain all current flagged messages as the conservative fallback.
3. Do not claim that original favorites were reconstructed.

## Focused and Other

Focused and Other are Inbox categories, not separate folders.

- Outlook search may return both categories even when one tab appears selected.
- Delete all of Other directly from the Other tab.
- When Other must follow age or keep rules, audit it separately instead of assuming Inbox search respects the category.
